How we organised

A headless team

  • No project manager, no team lead. Five equals working at the same time
  • Stilla supported the meetings: prepared agendas and discussion topics, summarised each session, and surfaced the open tickets
  • It did not run standups, assign the work, or own the backlog. We did that together
  • Everything stayed visible and explicit through GitHub PRs and Linear
Pelle. "Headless" means no PM role exists at all. The team self-organised as five equals. Stilla was a meeting aide, not a coordinator: it prepared agendas, summarised sessions, and surfaced tickets, but the team owned the standups, the assignments, and the backlog.

Summary
3 minutes. Pull the threads together before the closer.
Summary

What we learned

  • You can run a product team with AI doing the coordination layer
  • The constraint is not the AI's capability — it's your ability to structure work so AI can act on it
  • Modular teams and modular code reinforce each other
  • Token limits, ambiguity, and meeting-tool debates are still very human problems
The single most important line: the constraint is structure, not capability. If the audience remembers one thing, make it this.
Summary

One future scenario

  • 2027: a 3-person team ships a B2B SaaS product in 6 weeks
  • AI writes 60% of the code, 100% of the tickets, and runs the retrospectives
  • The humans do: taste, judgment, stakeholder relationships, and the demo
  • "The question isn't whether AI joins the team. It's whether your team knows how to work with it."
Deliver the closing line slowly. It's the bridge into the closer question.
Summary

If you want to try this

  • Define your through-line before you start building
  • Assign AI a real role with real responsibilities
  • Use Conway's Law intentionally: design team = design system
  • Build async-first: everything documented, nothing lost in calls
  • Plan for token limits like you plan for deadlines
  • Treat the presentation as part of the product
