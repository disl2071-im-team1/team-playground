How we organised

A headless team

  • No roles. No project manager, no team lead. Five people, equal, working at the same time.
  • We brought in an AI agent, Stilla, to orchestrate the work. It prepared agendas, summarised each session, and surfaced the open tickets.
  • An AI agent is one you give a standing job to: it acts on its own instead of waiting to be prompted each time.
  • We kept the rest ourselves. We ran our own check-ins, divided the work, and owned the backlog together.
Introduce Stilla as an AI agent before leaning on it, and gloss the term for newcomers. Human ownership is the active statement: we ran the check-ins, we owned the backlog. Linear and GitHub get defined on the Irreducible Stack slide, so we don't define them here.

How we organised

The Pull Request

  • A Pull Request is how one person proposes a change to the shared code.
  • You make the change on a copy, then open a request that says here is what I changed and why.
  • Before it becomes real, someone else reads it, can comment, and either merges it in or asks for changes.

Nothing reaches the live product without being seen. Anyone can propose, the change is visible, and it is recorded for good.

ANIMATED. Reveal the three steps one at a time, then the why. This is the honest mechanism of how we actually collaborate: propose, review, merge. Nothing ships unseen.

How we worked

We met once a week and worked async the rest of the time. Four principles held it together.
1
Be task-oriented.Let the work define the structure.
2
Keep a shared language.Common ground on goals, tools and standards.
3
Communicate a lot, and openly.Information reaches everyone, work is visibly divided.
4
Avoid single points of failure.Everything is doable by more than one person.
