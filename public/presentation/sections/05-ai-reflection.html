Design decision

Four directions. One call.

We each designed a direction. Stilla chose mine, for reasons we could not argue with. — Four of us each brought a design direction. Rather than debate it ourselves, we asked Stilla to choose against one thing that mattered most: can a stressed municipal officer read this at a glance. It picked Clean Path and named why: the cleanest visual hierarchy; the "issue or lift an advisory" panel as the most actionable pattern; colour-coded risk levels that work across ANY hazard; and the most approachable for a non-specialist audience. What's interesting is that Stilla didn't just pick on taste, it reasoned from the user and from reusability. That direction became the system that let four independently-built tabs feel like one product.
