Background

  • We were shown a set of future scenarios and asked to bring one to life.
  • We chose the one about an air-pollution warning system in Mumbai.
Just set the scene. Two sentences, then move on. Do not explain Clean Path yet, that is the next slide.
Framing

The brief

  • On its face: take a scenario, make it real, and evaluate it critically.
  • The turn: we did not build the Mumbai system. We followed open city data we could actually build on, and it became Clean Path.
  • The point: the brief was the springboard, not the subject. We focused on the How.

AI-native means the tools are not bolted on, they are how the team exists at all.

Honesty: the open data is Swedish (SMHI, HaV, pollen, Copernicus), not Mumbai. Say "open city data we could build on", do not imply Mumbai data was available. The brief got us moving, the How is what we kept.
Framing

The brief asked for a prototype. We built the operating model behind it.

  • We were placed as a thinktank: take a direction, make it real, and judge it honestly.
  • So we focused on the How, not the What. We built the operating model a five-person AI-native team runs on.
  • It produced an application, Clean Path. It could have been almost anything. The model is the point.
This is the spine of my whole section. The How is the takeaway. The model is the deliverable; Clean Path is just what it happened to produce.
